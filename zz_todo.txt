cave climb underneath. on_player_enter triggers on entering the bounds defined by me, so I 
can set the floor to the top of the cave level with my entrance. From either way, they'll be 
met with a welcome, some wall text, and maybe an item or sticker for getting through the 
little cave climb I made. At the front, there will be an open dojo area with a training dummy, 
either a sulfur cube or a mummy mannequin. I'll have a balloon giver set out for free not far 
from the entrance. Kits will be obtainable from another room off to the side; I'll have to 
adjust the armor stand summoning positions for it. Maybe players will be able to find stray 
kit items hidden throughout the pagoda, earning a sticker for it if they do. Then, there's the 
kits. Reading all their lore and figuring out how is worth a 3rd sticker. Within the booth, 
they can attack the dummy with the abilities, which might be worth another sticker if they use 
them all. Joining a Battlegrounds match and winning is definitely worth a sticker, making 5,
which is the total allowed per booth. The staff mentioned cycling each booth's combat game in
3-minute intervals. Others might have a certain arrangement of teams, but for Imperium, I 
imagine a free-for-all deathmatch for however many players will be present. I'll share a 
lobby with another booth for the Battlegrounds, and I imagine we'll both have our own sizable
kit rosters, so I might have my armor stands shrunken down in there with the Scale attribute.

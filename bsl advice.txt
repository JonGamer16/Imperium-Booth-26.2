    1. Booth Definition — the one required file
        Confirms your wiring items exactly:

        ticking_functions is a map like {"imperium:tick":"1t","imperium:5t":"5t"}; timings are strings, and these only run while a player is inside a bounding box. This validates your two-worlds split (check_stands → imperium:tick), and confirms your "Ticking targets: create imperium:tick and imperium:5t" item.
        on_player_enter must be a full function location (imperium:booth/enter_booth) — confirms that todo item. Global enter fires only on not-in-any-box → in-a-box; per-box enter/exit also supported (backs your cave per-box reward). (Minor: the doc's on_player_exit blurb has a copy-paste typo saying "enter" — it does mean exit.)
    2. Ticking Your Booth — [L243-269](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L243-L269)
        Answers your "Combat ticking method" open decision:

        Booth ticking_functions won't reach arena players (they're outside your bounds) — you were right.
        Two sanctioned escapes: Schedule Clocks for short bursts (schedule function … 5t replace), and a Persistent Booth Tick that runs every tick regardless of presence — but it's approved case-by-case and must be gated behind one cheap score/flag check. Since your cooldown engine is event-driven (damage advancements), you may need very little persistent ticking. Flag this one for your coordinator.
    3. Entity Tags — [L271-290](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L271-L290)
        summit.booth_entity.imperium on all entities (schematic ones auto-tagged) — confirms your tagging item.

        BSL gives you summit.in_booth.imperium on any player inside a box — you don't need to build your own "in booth" tracker.
        summit.enable_block_interactivity marker → new todo: if your build has doors/levers/GUI blocks that must stay usable, they'll be blocked by their worldguard system unless you place these markers.

    9. Battlegrounds API — [L819-931](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L819-L931)

        set_structure + the example arena structure file "in the BSL distribution" — that's your arena structure item.
        Scoreboards you get for free: $players summit.battlegrounds, $time_left summit.battlegrounds (seconds to next booth in the cycle — this is the 3-min cycling; your "set ends" = booth_inactive).

    10. Regen API — [L1067-1085](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L1067-L1085) — NEW, not in your todo
        Every player gets Resistance V + Saturation by default. Consequences:

        Players standing in your booth are effectively immune to ability damage — fine for the arena, but if you ever want in-booth sparring you'd need to tag summit.no_regen + effect clear.
        Battlegrounds players are auto-exempt from resistance (hunger untouched), so arena combat works — but you must design around no natural regen there, which fits your STOCK format.
        The training dummy is a mob, not a player, so this doesn't block damaging it — but it does mean players can't be hurt by the dummy demo in-booth.
    11. Custom Items — [L427-467](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L427-L467) — NEW, big implications
        By default, every item given in your booth is deleted when the player leaves the bounds. Your kit distributor hands out items in the booth; those vanish on exit. Kit items for the arena come via kit_function instead — but any hidden-item pickups or gifts you want players to keep need {summit:{persist:{}}}.
        Any summoned/dropped item is auto-processed by their item manager unless you add summit custom_data. This hits every thrown projectile you have — Smokey's throwing knife/dart, Livvy's web, Mummy's energy crystal — they'll need summit:{droppable:{ignore:true}} or a callback. Add an audit item for this.
        Items aren't droppable by default (trash cans provided); mark untrashable only with permission. Add a pack-mark lore line (booth name) to given items.
    12. Queue API — [L932-1065](.claude/Smithed API/Summit Handbook.md#L932-L1065) — clarifies a misconception
        Explicitly "SEPARATE FROM THE BATTLEGROUNDS. DO NOT USE THIS FOR THE BATTLEGROUNDS." Your "players TP in and queue a match" idea is handled by the Battlegrounds activation cycle itself, not this. Only touch Queue API if you add a non-combat queued activity.
